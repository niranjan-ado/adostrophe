Matterport Basics for Beginners

New to the world of 3D virtual tours? Start here to understand what Matterport is, how it works, and its fundamental benefits for showcasing your spaces.

Matterport is a leading platform for creating immersive 3D digital twins of physical spaces. It uses specialized cameras to capture comprehensive spatial data, which is then processed into a navigable 3D model. Benefits include enhanced marketing, remote collaboration, improved operational efficiency, and data visualization for various industries like real estate, hospitality, construction, and retail.

Our process is simple: 1. **Consultation:** We discuss your needs and project scope. 2. **On-site Scan:** Our technicians use Matterport Pro cameras to scan your space efficiently. 3. **Processing:** The captured data is processed into a high-fidelity 3D model. 4. **Delivery:** You receive a link to your completed virtual tour, ready to embed on your website or share.

Scan time depends on the size and complexity of the space. A typical residential property might take 1-2 hours, while a large commercial venue could take several hours or even a full day. After the scan, processing usually takes 24-48 hours, and your final tour link will be delivered shortly thereafter.

Mattertags are interactive hotspots embedded directly into your 3D virtual tour. They can contain text descriptions, images, videos, or links to external websites. They're excellent for highlighting features, providing product information, adding historical context, or guiding users through specific points of interest.

Yes! Matterport tours can be viewed in virtual reality, offering an even deeper level of immersion. Users with compatible VR headsets can 'walk through' your space as if they were physically there, providing an unparalleled viewing experience.

Matterport: Advanced Client Questions & Best Practices

For clients looking to leverage Matterport for advanced applications, BIM integration, large-scale projects, and strategic insights, this section provides detailed answers.

Yes, absolutely! Matterport scans can be a powerful tool for BIM workflows. We can provide floor plans, point cloud data (.xyz, .pts), and reflected ceiling plans (RCP) derived directly from your 3D model. This data can be imported into CAD and BIM software (like AutoCAD, Revit) for accurate measurements, as-built documentation, and design validation.

For large and complex spaces (e.g., multi-floor buildings, expansive event venues, industrial facilities), we employ advanced scanning strategies. This includes strategic scan point placement, leveraging multiple Matterport Pro cameras simultaneously if needed, and careful data stitching to ensure a seamless and comprehensive digital twin. Our team specializes in efficient capture for challenging environments.

To ensure the best scan quality and efficiency, we recommend: decluttering, ensuring good lighting (open blinds, turn on all lights), removing sensitive information or personal items, and making sure all areas to be scanned are accessible and clean. Think of it like preparing for a professional photoshoot.

We prioritize your privacy. During scanning, we advise removing or blurring sensitive information (e.g., whiteboards with proprietary data, personal photos). Matterport's platform uses secure cloud storage, and access to your models is controlled by you. We can also provide guidance on blurring faces or specific objects post-scan if required.

Matterport models provide excellent relative accuracy for interior measurements (e.g., room dimensions, door widths) typically within 1% of reality, which is highly reliable for planning, layout, and virtual staging. While not survey-grade, they are highly sufficient for most architectural, real estate, and facilities management needs. For specific, highly precise measurements for construction, we can discuss additional services like laser scanning.

360 Photography for Google Street View

Unlock local visibility with immersive 360 photos directly integrated into your Google Business Profile. A simple yet effective way to showcase your space.

360 photography for Google Street View involves capturing immersive panoramic images that are directly uploaded to your Google Business Profile. These images can be stitched together to create a navigable virtual experience, allowing customers to explore your business directly from Google Search and Maps.

While both offer virtual walkthroughs, 360 photography for GSV is a simpler, more cost-effective solution. It provides a series of connected panoramic images for basic navigation (clicking 'chevrons' and panning). Matterport tours, on the other hand, create a full 3D 'dollhouse' view, precise measurements, and advanced interactive features like Mattertags, offering a much richer and more detailed immersive experience.

No! One of the key advantages of 360 photography for Google Street View is that the images are hosted directly by Google on their Maps platform. This means there are no ongoing hosting fees from our side, making it a highly economical option for businesses.

When multiple 360 images are strategically captured and linked within Google Street View, users can click on the white arrow 'chevrons' that appear on the screen to virtually move from one point to another within your space. Coupled with the ability to click and drag to pan around, this creates a compelling virtual tour experience directly within Google's ecosystem.

Enhancing your Google Business Profile with 360 imagery significantly boosts engagement. It increases your visibility in local search, builds trust with potential customers by showcasing your interior, and can lead to more walk-ins and inquiries. Businesses with virtual tours on their listings are often seen as more reputable and inviting.

Google Ad Grants for Non-Profits

Amplify your mission with up to $10,000/month in free Google advertising. Our expert management ensures compliance and maximum impact for your cause.

Google Ad Grants is a program by Google that provides eligible non-profit organizations with up to $10,000 USD in in-kind advertising every month to promote their missions on Google Search. It's a powerful tool to drive awareness, attract volunteers, solicit donations, and reach a wider audience without incurring direct advertising costs.

Eligibility for Google Ad Grants primarily requires that your organization holds valid charity status in your respective country (e.g., 80G/12A in India) and meets specific Google guidelines, including having a high-quality website and engaging in non-discriminatory practices. Educational institutions, governmental entities, and hospitals are typically not eligible.

Managing Google Ad Grants is more complex than standard Google Ads due to stringent compliance requirements and lower average bid limits. Without experienced professionals, accounts often underperform or even get suspended. Expertise is needed to navigate policy guidelines, maintain account activity, optimize for conversions within bid constraints, and ensure maximum utilization of the grant budget.

Recognizing the vital work of non-profits and the budget constraints of small businesses, Adostrophe is committed to making our 20 years of premium SEM expertise accessible. We offer our Google Ad Grants management services at **low or no cost** for eligible NGOs and carefully selected small businesses, ensuring you can leverage this powerful tool effectively without a significant financial burden.
