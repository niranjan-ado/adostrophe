Preparing a House for a Matterport Virtual Tour

If a house for sale or rent is scheduled for a Matterport virtual tour, here’s how it’s prepared before we arrive on site. Read checklist:

By Adostrophe | Dec 15, 2023 | Matterport, Real Estate, Residential Real Estate

If you're getting ready to sell or rent your home, a **Matterport virtual tour** can be a game-changer. These immersive 3D experiences allow potential buyers or renters to walk through your property from anywhere, giving them a true sense of the space. To ensure your home shines in its best light, a little preparation goes a long way.

This guide provides a comprehensive checklist to help you prep your house before our technicians arrive, along with general guidelines for those new to Matterport.

---

Why Matterport Virtual Tours?

Matterport virtual tours offer significant advantages for residential real estate:

  • Engaging Promotion: Showcase your home in a dynamic, interactive way that goes beyond static photos.
  • Wider Reach: Attract potential buyers or renters from anywhere in the world, available 24/7.
  • Time Savings: Reduce the number of physical showings by allowing interested parties to pre-qualify themselves.
  • High-Quality Visuals: Matterport captures high-fidelity images and accurate spatial data.
  • Versatile Output: Beyond the virtual tour, we can extract **2D images** for traditional real estate listings on platforms like 99Acres. Unlike standard photos, Matterport captures multiple perspectives from each scan point, offering a more comprehensive view.

While you can create virtual tours with smartphones or 360-degree cameras, professional scanners like Matterport's Pro 2, Pro 3, and Leica BLK360 G1 offer the **precise measurements** essential for an accurate and professional presentation.

---

What to Expect During a Virtual Tour Scan

Once you're ready, we'll coordinate the details and schedule your scan. Our **Matterport capture technicians** will arrive on-site and begin the process, which typically involves:

  • Setting Up: The camera is placed on a tripod and starts a 360-degree scan. Each rotation takes about 30 seconds.
  • Scanning Process: The technician will strategically move the camera to new positions, capturing hundreds of data points depending on your property's size and complexity. Areas with reflective surfaces like large windows might take a bit longer.
  • Coverage: For very large homes, the process might require multiple days. It's crucial to minimize interruptions like excessive foot traffic, as this can affect scanning duration and quality. However, don't worry about pets; animals and birds are fine and can add a natural element to the images!
---

The Post-Scan Process

After the scanning is complete, the captured data is uploaded to Matterport's secure servers for processing. Smaller spaces usually take a few hours, while larger models may require a day or two.

  • Editing: Our team organizes the scan points, adds interactive elements like **Mattertags** (information points), labels, and a **highlight reel** to enhance the viewer's experience.
  • Delivery: Once editing is finalized, we provide you with the unique URL and embed code for your virtual tour. We can also help you embed the tour seamlessly onto your website, ensuring it's fully responsive across all devices.
---

Property Preparation Checklist for Your House

To ensure the smoothest possible scanning process and the best final tour, please prepare your property as follows. Our technicians won't adjust items on-site, so a little effort beforehand makes a big difference. This list serves as a **recommendation**, not a strict mandate.

General Cleaning & Organization

  • Floors: Give all floors, including patios and balconies, a thorough clean. Polish tiles and mop surfaces. A detailed clean-up benefits a virtual tour that can be used for years.
  • Reflective Surfaces: Clean all glass windows, mirrors, and steel handles to remove any smudges or fingerprints.
  • Monitors: Ensure all TV and computer screens are clean. If they are on, set them to display a static image, like a neutral background or screensaver.
  • Shelves: Make sure all shelves and visible surfaces are dust-free.
  • Personal Items: Hide or lock away any personal items like mail, bills, medications, or valuable electronics. Neatly placed laptops without stickers are generally fine. Remove or hide charging cables.
  • Garbage Bins: Remove or hide all garbage bins from view in all rooms, including kitchens and bathrooms.

Bathrooms

  • Dry Floors: Ensure bathroom floors are completely dry.
  • Toilet Seats: Put all toilet seats down.
  • Towels: Fold and hang towels neatly.

Exterior

  • Exterior Floors: If you have stone floors or patios in villas, consider giving them a water wash.

Lighting & Ambiance

  • Turn On All Lights: Switch on every light fixture, including lamps, exterior lights, and even kitchen chimney lights (ensure fans are off). Electronic items with indicator lights should also be on.
  • Ceiling Fans: All ceiling fans should be *off* to prevent blur in the scans.
  • Windows and Curtains: Open all windows and curtains wide to maximize natural light. This makes the space look more inviting and spacious.
  • Override Motion Detectors: If you have motion-activated lights, override them to stay on throughout the scan.
---

The Excitement of a Virtual Tour

Here are two final points to keep in mind:

  1. Presence During Scan: Often, we're given keys by security personnel, and we don't necessarily need the homeowner present. In such cases, we'll turn on all lights and open windows for the scan. Once complete, we'll close them and return the keys. Any other cleaning or organizing remains outside our scope.
  2. Perfection vs. Authenticity: While there's no upper limit to how much you can clean and stage, don't obsess over every tiny detail. If perfection isn't achievable, embrace the "natural" or "charming" aspects of your home.

Take our word for it—a **Matterport tour for your house** will look fantastic regardless. These checklists are merely guidelines to help optimize the outcome. When we say "can't," it's more of a "not recommended." For instance, while we *can* scan with people walking around, those areas might appear blurry.

A Matterport virtual tour can significantly help **sell or rent a house faster** by offering an unparalleled virtual walkthrough experience. These preparation tips are designed to help you get the most out of your tour and ensure a high-quality, authentic presentation.
